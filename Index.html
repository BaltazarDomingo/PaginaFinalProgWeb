    Lando Norris

    Born 13 November 1999 in Bristol, United Kingdom, to Adam and Cisca Norris. He is the second oldest alongside three siblings, with two younger sisters Flo and Cisca, and an older brother Oliver.

    He started his racing career at the age of seven when he claimed pole position at his first karting national event and in 2014, Norris made his car racing debut in the Ginetta Junior Championship.

    His road to F1 begun in October 2016 when he made a guest appearance in the final round of the European Formula 3 Championship, which took him to racing full-time with Carlin in the 2017 edition. He then went on to racing full-time in the 2018 FIA Formula 2 Championship where he ended 2nd. After being the McLaren oficial test and reserve driver during 2018, he was signed as an oficial driver by McLaren for the 2019 Formula One World Championship. Since then, Norris has been part of 5 podiums, and has finished 6th in the drivers championship of the 2021 Formula One World Championship.

    Daniel Ricciardo

    Born on 1 July 1989 in Perth, Western Australia, to Giuseppe and Grace Ricciardo. Ricciardo also has a sister; Michelle

    Ricciardo started karting at the age of nine and entered numerous karting events. During the mid-part of the 2008 season, Ricciardo made his Formula Three début at the Nürburgring and then moved to the British Formula 3 Championship for the 2009 season

    Ricciardo made his track debut at the wheel of a Formula One car, when he tested for Red Bull Racing at the young drivers test in december 2009. After being a reserve and test driver for all of 2010 and half of 2011, he was contracted to Hispania Racing as an oficial driver for what was left of that 2011 Formula One World Championship. Since then, he has been a part of Scuderia Toro Rosso through 2012 and 2013, then moved to a powerful Infinity Red Bull Racing for the next four years. In 2019 he moved to Renault F1 Team and competed for them for two years. Finally, for the 2021 season, he moved to McLaren where he has been ever since. Ricciardo has acomplished 32 podiums and 8 race wins, one of them being at McLaren (Monza 2021).
